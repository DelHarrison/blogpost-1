Why UX Research is important

by Delaney Harrison

Posted on February 4, 2020

Why UX Research?

User research is the initial stage and one of the most significant activities of a product-design cycle. All of your team’s hard work, time, and money will be for nothing if you end up making something that nobody wants to use. User research should always come before UX strategy because it helps eliminate unwarranted assumptions from the design process. It also helps you to find the right people to enable you to move your product forward, make any necessary changes, and continue iterating on your product design. UX Research is essential, don't let anyone tell you any different!

By using various user-research techniques during this phase, you can better understand users and their needs, which helps your team to identify product requirements.

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Focus Groups
  • Surveys
  • Contextual Inquiry
  • Card Sorting
  • Journey Mapping
  • The Creation of Personas and Scenarios
  • Participatory Design
  • Joint Application Development (JAD) sessions
  • A/B testing
  • Design Critiques
  • Eyetracking
  • Usability Testing
